description: |
  Techniques for 3D modeling are advancing rapidly, and the biodiversity collections community will benefit from keeping abreast of the latest advances and thinking strategically about the new opportunities that 3D modeling offers.  Here, we will present experimentation with recently developed methods (e.g., Neural Radiance Fields and 3D Gaussian Splatting), advances in robotics and macrophotography that provide scaling opportunities for model generation, and new analyses made possible with 3D modeling. These approaches have the potential to dramatically reduce digitization time while increasing fidelity and throughput across a wide range of specimen types. We will also discuss practical considerations for adoption, including infrastructure requirements, workflow integration, and sustainability.
